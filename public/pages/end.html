Now it’s your move!

Break free from addictive scrolling. Here are some tools to start with
↓ ↓ ↓

Each scene marks a step in the descent into addictive scrolling, showing how the brain changes along the way and the consequences that follow.

1. Discovery

→ First glance

The first exposure often happens in moments of boredom or downtime. The phone becomes a quick source of stimulation, filling empty spaces in the day. This shows how easily the habit begins, small, harmless at first, but it plants the seed.

2. Habit

→ Routine Stop

The habit forms quickly when the phone becomes the very first thing checked in the morning. This scene highlights how scrolling starts integrating into daily routines, shaping automatic behaviors and setting the tone for the day.

3. Distraction

→ Lost Focus

The phone interrupts concentration and creates constant distraction. This illustrates how scrolling begins to weaken focus, dividing attention between tasks and endless notifications. The brain starts to crave quick rewards instead of sustained effort.

4. Disturbed Sleep

→ Night Shift

Late-night scrolling disrupts sleep cycles and overstimulates the brain. The blue light and constant input prevent rest, symbolized by the brain overloaded with posts. This stage shows how the addiction starts to affect physical and mental health directly.

5. Isolation

→ Dead end

At the end of the spiral, social media no longer provides joy only emptiness and isolation. The room symbolizes disconnection from the real world, friends, and meaningful activities. It represents the emotional void created when scrolling becomes the only source of stimulation.
